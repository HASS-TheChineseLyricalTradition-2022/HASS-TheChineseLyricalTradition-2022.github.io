Research

Wang Wei (ca. 699-761)

Wang Wei was known as a poetic genius, using simple words and verses to express his visions. Besides his poetry, Wang Wei was also famous of his monochrome landscape paintings. His works have great influence in the arts and culture of many generations of artists and writers after his time.

禪

Chan Buddhism

Wang Wei drew inspiration from the Chan Buddhist notions of sparsity and emptiness, that the self is conditioned by external forces. His arts therefore represent and express these ideas, by using simple strokes and language to describe the vast landscape.

Wang Stream Collection

Wang Wei's most famous single work is the "Wang Stream Collection", which consists of twenty poems of the scenery at different locations of his Wangchuan estate. Each poem is written with extreme symplicity and are consistent, with four lines of five chinese characters to describe the scene.

輞川集

Although his estate has changed and do not represent the landscape which Wang Wei describes in his paintings and poetry, his works allow us to envision and imagine the beauty and ambience of the various spots of the Wang Chuan Villa.

Shadow Light Box

Landscape Painting Choice

We have chosen this landscape painting as our choice to display in our Shadow Light Box. This image potrays Wang Wei at the scenic deer park of his estate, as he writes the poem about it. The landscape features the vast empty mountains with no people in sight, which expresses the seclusion that Wang Wei experiences in his retreat.

Processing the landscape image to monochrome

The team believes that a monochrome painting best suits the lyrical vision found in the poetry of Wang Wei. His paintings are monochrome too. The visual medium of the shadow light box aids to accentuate the elements in a monochrome image. Thus, the landscape painting generated is further processed into monochrome to bring out the details of the image.

The Shadow Light Box

The shadow light box brings the lyrical vision of Wang Wei into the modern era with modern materials such as 3D-Printing and the use of LED lights to accentuate the type of lighting he would have seen during his time in the Tang Dynasty.

3D Modelling

The base of the lightbox is fully designed in the CAD environment by us.

3D Printing

The 3D model is then fabricated using 3D printing.

Toggle Switch

The lightbox has 2 modes that can turn the 3D image between the whiteness of daylight, and the warmth of dusk with the flick of a switch.

The 5 layered Acrylic panels with different cutouts of the image represents what Wang Wei’s River Estate in his Wang River Collection might look like. In the layers we can clearly see the mountains in the background, leading to a stream of water and finally onto Wang Wei himself, the main lyrical protagonist, standing outside his estate, admiring the scenery and thinking about his next poem.

The Battery Cover has Wang Wei’s Poem, Lu Chai, laser engraved onto wood in a calligraphic manner, depicting the 3D scene of the deserted hills he so describes.
